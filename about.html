Reinvent yourself

To paraphrase Mike Tyson,

Every woman has a plan until her first child is born.

The glory years, part un

I used to be a hotshot in my early 20s - for starters, I rubbed elbows with the finance minister of Hungary. Not because of the nepotism of contacts: it was a direct consequence of leading research on the effects of flat tax on the Hungarian economy. I kept conducting further research on finance and taxes, have been active in the Ruby community, gave the valedictorian speech at my university, etc.

Welcome to the Jungle

Then came the children - three of them. The first one never saw the light, thanks to the ‘fantastic’ counsel of some doctors (‘nature took out the trash’ is what he literally said.).

But two of them did - and they are the most precious, awesome kids I know, a constant source of joy and excitement.

Short after the girls were born, we sold everything that didn’t fit into a few suitcases and lived all over the place. This self-imposed sabbatical was one of the most rewarding things in my life so far: immersing ourselves into foreign cultures, surrounded by exotic experiences, watching our kids absorbing the world around them as they grew up was pure bliss!

They also (unwillingly) shattered my dreams, wholly and utterly obliterating my plans, forcing me on a path I would have never taken in my pre-mother days.

Would I hit the ‘undo’ button if life came with that feature? Not.

Do I love every day I spend with them? Absolutely!

That doesn’t change the fact that I got isolated from everyone I knew in my previous professional life, and I don’t just mean finance-minister level figures, but everyone. My budding career came to a screeching halt, my achievements forgotten, my notebooks full of ideas and plans collecting dust in my mom’s basement.

That's the effect of getting out of sight entirely for a few years (more like a full decade in my case).

I can’t say I’m not bitter a teeny bit, regretful even, thinking about all the could-have-beens.

Negative emotions are not the point of this monologue, though.

The glory years, part deux

The point is, that:

I am back.

With a vengeance.

Time to dust off those notebooks.

Time to catch up for the missed opportunities.

Time to reinvent myself.

Time to start making.

Time to start helping others doing the same.

We are full of ideas (who isn’t?!), but instead of just banging them out and see what sticks, we are turning the journey into a product itself.

Speaking of products: we are also meticulously documenting the processes, thinking, design, implementation, marketing and every other aspect of creating them. Being off the map for years is actually a blessing in disguise: we are very well aware what are we missing and thus we are well positioned to cater to new bootstrappers/makers.

We could say that we are meta-bootstrapping: bootstrapping future bootstrappers; and not just any bootstrappers, but specifically women, with focus on Africa, Asia, and Eastern-Europe.

Why women? Because women are awesome - and underrepresented, undervalued, underappreciated and not receiving nearly enough visibility. It’s a shame, as they are bringing unique powers, vision, and qualities to the table. Why have +-10% of awesomeness when we can have more, hopefully WAY more?!

To put our money where our mouth is, we just started working on the branding of SHEmagine.com, a community and platform for up-and-coming femtrepreneurs and female bootstrappers, giving them an opportunity to showcase their work, educating, mentoring and helping them however we can (financially as well - part of our profits will go towards scholarships/funding exciting projects).

Also, the ‘kids’ I keep mentioning are two girls - we will do everything in our power to build a world where women are not second-class citizens, but respected and equal peers to men. We are creating SHEmagine for them too!
